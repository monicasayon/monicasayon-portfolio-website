Virginia Woolf

A timeless novelist

Virginia Woolf was born in South Kensington, London, into an affluent and intellectual family as the seventh child of Julia Prinsep Jackson and Leslie Stephen.

Here's a time line of Dr. Borlaug's life:

  • 1882– Born Adeline Virginia Stephen on January 25 in London, England.

  • 1895– Mother, Julia Stephen, dies suddenly. Virginia, age 13, experiences her first mental breakdown.

  • 1904– Father, Sir Leslie Stephen, dies. Virginia has another breakdown and is briefly institutionalized.

  • 1905– Begins writing for the Times Literary Supplement and becomes part of the Bloomsbury Group, an influential circle of writers, artists, and intellectuals.

  • 1912– Marries Leonard Woolf, a political theorist and writer.

  • 1915– Publishes her first novel, The Voyage Out.

  • 1917– Co-founds Hogarth Press with Leonard Woolf, which would later publish many of her own works and those of other major writers like T.S. Eliot and Sigmund Freud.

  • 1925– Publishes Mrs. Dalloway, one of her most celebrated novels, exploring themes of time, consciousness, and mental illness.

  • 1927– Releases To the Lighthouse, often considered her masterpiece.

  • 1928– Publishes Orlando, a groundbreaking and playful novel inspired by her close relationship with Vita Sackville-West.

  • 1929– Releases A Room of One's Own, a powerful feminist essay arguing for women's financial independence and space to create.

  • 1931– Publishes The Waves, a highly experimental novel written in poetic soliloquies.

  • 1941– Amid fears of a mental breakdown and the pressures of World War II, Virginia Woolf dies by suicide on March 28 by drowning herself in the River Ouse. She was 59 years old.
